Sample Itinerary

Tour Historical Places in 1 Day

Time What to do
10:00AM-12:30PM

Tour at Geongbokgung (Palace)
If you have more time, make sure to check out nearby hanbok-rental places so that you can try on traditional Korean clothes and tour the Geongbokgung.
You would feel like you have become the royal member of Joseon Dynasty.
Hanbok is extremely photogenic clothes as well. It will definitely make your tour more unique.

12:30PM-2:00PM

Lunch at Onjium, Fine dining Korea Restaurant
Make sure to book ahead to be able to dine in this restaurant.
The restaurant is walking distance from the Gyungbok Palace, so you can walk to your lunch place.
2:30PM-3:30PM

Tour at Changdeokgung (Palace) and Jongmyo Shrine
You can tour Changdeokgung and Jongmyo Shrine at the same time because Jongmyo Shrine is actually located inside the Changdeokgung. The beauty of Changdeokgung is quiet different from Gyeongbokgung. Go ahead and see how different two places are!

3:30PM-5:30PM

Explore Bukchon Hanok Village
Bukchon Hanok Village is a neighborhood surrounding the two places: Geongbokgung and Changdeokgung. Majority of the buildings are built in the for of Hanok, which is a traditional Korean houses. Moreover, all of the store signs are in Korean, which is very unique. The village is full of cute boutique shops so make sure to check it out.

6:30PM-8:00PM

Eat Dinner around Ikseon-dong Hanok Street. Another neighborhood near the palaces are Ikseon-dong Hanok Street. Similar to the Bukchon Hanok Village, the stores and houses are built in the shape of Hanok. The street is full of amazing restaurants including Tokyo bingsu, Cafe Sik-mool, and Changhwadang.
