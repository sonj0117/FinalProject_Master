Geongbokgung (Palace)

Geoungbokgung was the main royal place of the Joseon Dynasty. It is the largest palace among the Five Grand Palaces built in Joseon dynasty. Geoungbokgung, however, was destroyed by the fire during Imjin War Japan. Current Geoungbokgung was reconstructed in the 19th century.
Geoungbokgung is extremely intricate and beautiful.They have nighttime viewing during spring, which is mesmerizing. Make sure to make it to nighttime viewing if you are visiting in the Spring!

Opearting Hours Mon to Sun: 9AM-6PM except Tuesday (Not Open)

Changdeokgung (Palace)

Changdeokgung is also one of the Five Grand Palaces built in the Joseon Dynasty. It was the most favored palace of the Joseon Kings and Queens. Changdeokgung served as the primary royal residence during the Joseon Dynasty. It is considered as the most beautiful palace in Korea. The palace was constructed to create perfect harmony with the topography. Changdeokgung's secret garden shos the perfect harmony with nature. In order to view the secret garden, you must buy the tickets in advance!

Opearting Hours Tues to Sun: 9AM-6PM

Jongmyo Shrine

Jongmyo is the oldest royal shrines in Korea. Jongmyo was also built in Joseon Dynasty for the purpose of performing ancestral rites for the deceased Kings and Queens.The original form of the shrine has been preserved since the Joseon Dynasty. You must by the tickets to enter.

Opearting Hours Mon to Sun: 9AM-6PM except Tuesday (Not Open) Feb - May 09:00 - 18:00 (Ticket Booth 09:00 - 17:00) Jun - Aug 09:00 - 18:30 (Ticket Booth 09:00 - 17:30) Sept - Oct 09:00 - 18:00 (Ticket Booth 09:00 - 17:00) Nov - Jan 09:00 - 17:30 (Ticket Booth 09:00 - 16:30)
